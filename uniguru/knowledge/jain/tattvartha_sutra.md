The Tattvartha Sutra is the "Manual of Jaina Principles". 
It begins with the famous sutra: "Samyag-darshana-jnana-charitrani mokshamargah" 
(Right faith, right knowledge, and right conduct constitute the path to liberation).

The text describes the seven Tattvas (realities):
1. Jiva (Soul)
2. Ajiva (Non-soul)
3. Asrava (Inflow of karma)
4. Bandha (Bondage of karma)
5. Samvara (Stoppage of karma)
6. Nirjara (Shedding of karma)
7. Moksha (Liberation)
